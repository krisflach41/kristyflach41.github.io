Who This Loan Type Is Typically Best For

When This Loan May Not Be the Best Fit

How Non-QM Loans Fit Into the Mortgage Landscape

Non-Qualified Mortgage loans serve borrowers whose financial profiles fall outside traditional underwriting formulas. These loans recognize that financial stability can exist even when income documentation or tax structures do not fit conventional boxes.

Lenders evaluate a broader picture of financial strength, including assets, cash flow patterns, and real-world income structures.

What Makes Non-QM Financing Different

Non-QM loans provide structural flexibility. Income may be verified through bank statements, asset reserves, or alternative documentation rather than tax returns alone.

This flexibility expands access to financing while maintaining responsible lending standards.

How Qualification Is Evaluated

  • Bank statement or alternative income verification
  • Strong equity or down payment position
  • Asset reserves and liquidity
  • Responsible credit management

Common Types of Non-QM Loan Structures

  • Bank Statement Loans
  • Asset Depletion Loans
  • Interest-Only Loans
  • Investor Cash-Flow Loans
  • Expanded Criteria or Credit Event Programs
